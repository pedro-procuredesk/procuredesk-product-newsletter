📊 Aging Dashboard · Enhanced

The Invoice Aging dashboard now acts, not just reports

See what’s overdue, drill into it, and pay it — all from one screen.

Pay › Invoices › Aging

Until now, the Aging dashboard could tell you what was overdue — but the moment you wanted to do something about it, you had to leave the page, find the invoice, and start over. The reporting and the acting lived in two different places.

"I can see what’s overdue, narrow it to one supplier, and pay it without opening another screen."

Two widgets now sit above the invoice table and stay in sync with it — here’s what each one does.

Aging buckets
Unpaid invoices grouped by due date — Overdue, 0–7, 7–14, 14–30, 30+ days — with the total payable in each.
  
Supplier exposure
Your largest unpaid balances, scoped to whichever aging bucket you’ve selected.
Synced filters
Pick a bucket and a supplier together to drill to something like “Amazon invoices due in the next 7 days.”
  
Pay from the dashboard
Select invoices and mark them paid or create a payment batch without switching screens.

● Set It Up This Week

3 things to try this week

All three are live in your account right now — each takes just a couple of minutes.

01 Switch Purchase Requests to the action view. On the Purchase Requests page, toggle from the table to the new action view — open requests, your pending tasks, and your frequently ordered items and most-used vendors all surface automatically in their own widgets, so you spend less time filtering.
02 Open an item’s transaction history. Under Inventory → Item Details, check the new Transaction History ledger — every receipt, release, adjustment, and transfer, each with a running balance so you can see exactly when a stock level changed.
03 Confirm your vendor remittance emails. In Settings → Email Notifications → Vendor → Payment Notifications, check the daily paid-invoice summary to vendors is on (it’s on by default) — it cuts down the “has this been paid?” follow-ups to your AP team.

Need help configuring any of these? Email our support team →

Available on request

Pay · Statement Matching

Vendor Statement Matching

Upload a vendor statement — Excel, CSV, or PDF — and ProcureDesk matches each line against the supplier invoices already in the system, flagging anything that doesn’t reconcile. PDF statements are read with OCR, so there’s no manual re-keying, and you can export the matched and unmatched results to Excel for audit.

Watch the walkthrough →  ·  Email support
💳

Expenses · Card Statement Matching

Credit Card Statement Matching

Upload a credit card statement (CSV or Excel) and every transaction is matched to an existing expense and tagged Exact Match, Partial Match, or Missing, so gaps are obvious. Filter by status to work through big monthly statements fast, then export the results with their matched expense references.

Watch the walkthrough →  ·  Email support
📋

Contracts · Bulk Import

Import Historical Contracts

Bring your whole contract library in at once: upload a CSV/XLS of contract details plus a ZIP of the signed PDFs, and ProcureDesk maps each PDF to its record by filename. Anything with missing or mismatched data lands in draft with the gaps flagged, so nothing fails silently.
